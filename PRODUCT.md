# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Readers of literary-leaning speculative fiction (fans of Pratchett, Scalzi, Martha Wells, The Expanse) who arrive via a shared story link, social media, or search. Secondary audience: editors, anthology curators, and agents evaluating T. H. Mercer professionally. Both audiences need the site to read as credible and unhurried, not salesy.

## Product Purpose

Showcase T. H. Mercer's short fiction and essays, and convert readers into mailing-list subscribers. The primary path is: land on a free story ("Pest Control") or the homepage feature, read, then subscribe via the reader-magnet flow for future work. A secondary commercial goal is driving sales of the anthology *Moral Arithmetic*. Success = subscriptions and anthology sales, not pageviews or time-on-site for its own sake.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, warm, humane — and precise, spare, confident. The fiction itself lives in the tension between cold systems and the people caught inside them; the site's design should hold that same tension: understated and literary, never gushing, but not clinical or cold either. Voice is direct and unpretentious (see About page: "Former engineer. Occasional cyborg. Storyteller.").

## Anti-references

- Pulp/genre-fiction sites: busy hero banners, stock sci-fi imagery, loud typography, garish color.
- SaaS marketing gloss: gradient text, hero-metric templates, eyebrow labels, identical card grids, numbered feature scaffolding.
- Anything that reads as a "content funnel" rather than an author's own space.

## Design Principles

- Restraint over spectacle — the writing is the product; design should get out of its way and let it read well (65-75ch prose, generous line-height, minimal chrome).
- One clear next action per page — every page ends in a single obvious step (read the next story, subscribe, buy the anthology), never competing CTAs.
- Warmth without cliché — literary and human, not pulp-genre and not SaaS-templated.
- Professional-grade at a glance — an editor or agent landing cold should read the site as legitimate within seconds.
- Every visual choice should hold the same tension the fiction does: quiet surface, precise underneath.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard WCAG AA baseline: ≥4.5:1 body text contrast, ≥3:1 large text, visible focus-visible states, skip-link, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for any motion. No unusual constraints beyond AA; keep doing what the current CSS already does well (skip link, focus rings, reduced-motion-safe transitions).
